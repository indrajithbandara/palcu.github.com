InfoEducatie is a national IT contest, which takes place every year at Galaciuc, Vrancea. It is a place where high-school hackers gather, to present their projects to juries from different universities or companies (Google, Amazon)

My project

PeopleSeparator - it is a web application, which groups people, based on their social profile. For the moment it only uses the common friends and common likes from Facebook to calculate how close can a human be to you.

The idea came when I was reading The Facebook Effect by David Kirkpatrick, and saw that Mark Zuckerberg saw Facebook first as an utility for the world. Like a demo, I created a website for an IT camp (TechFriendCamp), and implemented the separator to it. I'll write a full detailed article about it, after I finish this series about my holiday.

Local phase

Well, I worked till 8 a.m. in the morning, and the contest was at 9 a.m. I tried to deploy the app at 8:30, but... it didn't work (I didn't have some dependencies installed). So I took my desktop computer to the presentation of the project.

All things were good, I had my "portable" tower with me, but when I had to present I saw that I didn't have the correct video jack: my nVidia video card has 2 DVI exists, where as the projector had an VGA entrance... so I took a taxi home, and brought the adaptor. But, the jury (made out of teachers) wasn't even paying attention to my project. So, I took 4th place and I didn't qualify (at a difference of 0,25 points of 10).

National contest

But I went to the national phase, because the guy on the 3rd place retired. So, after a 12-hour journey by train, with a stop in Bucharest, me, Ionut and Bogdan arrive in Focsani. Then, we took a bus, because Galaciuc is situated in a remote forest.

There, all the contestants were coding. We were allowed to fine-tune our apps for one day. So everybody was debugging, or adding extra-lines to their apps, in the pavilion: it was the only place were we had internet... at about 2 KB/s (when it was not windy). And the other problem were the power cords... there were not enough holes to plug them in... so if you ever come to Galaciuc, an extension cord is a must.

But the general atmosphere is very cool: 100 geeks, every one of them with a laptop, coding in different languages and talking about the same things. We even had  a quiz night, when we stayed until 3 a.m. and answered different logic puzzles played pantomime.

Workshops

All day long we had some excellent workshops, some of the best were (all in Romanian):
  • HTML5? HTML5! - the basics of HTML5 by Sabin Buraga
  • Pagini web mai rapide - some tricks used by Amazon & others by Alex Burciu
  • De la validarea formularelor Web la porcii verzi - finite automata in a funny way by Sabin Buraga
  • Focus 1: Construirea unui produs functional, utilizabil si intuitiv & Focus 2: Cand psihologia se intalneste cu designul interactiunii unui produs software - UI, UX and psychology for web, explained by Vali Bora
  • Javascript! Do you speak it?  & Developing games for mobile devices - first is an intro in Javascript (and we discussed about the future of it) and second was the story of a game for ImagineCup built on Windows Phone 7 (and we had a debate on mobile platforms)
  • Cum sa construiesti un "mini" Google - why to use Information Retrieval in your app by Bogdan Gaza
  • Python Intro - at 11 p.m, Mihai Oaida made a presentation such that if you didn't know anything about Python, after you saw how "geeky and beautiful" it is, the next day you would read the whole manual and start coding in it.
  • Sfaturi pentru o prezentare reusita - a practical introduction in presentations by Mihai Oaida and Silviu Vert
